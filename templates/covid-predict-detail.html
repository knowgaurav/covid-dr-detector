Detect COVID-19

Project Brief:

COVID-19 is an acronym for coronavirus disease 2019 or SARS-coronavirus-2 which was first discovered in December 2019 in Wuhan, China. Since then, it has been rapidly spreading all over the world and infecting not only humans but other animals too with ACE2 receptors in their lungs. Even though the fatality rate of COVID-19 is lower than previous coronavirus diseases like MERS or SARS, the transmissibility rate is far higher. This makes early detection and diagnosis of COVID-19 crucial to limit the spread from infected people. It also helps in the treatment and management of infected individuals. Developing countries with much of their population living in villages like India and Brazil have been affected far greater than developed countries with adequate healthcare infrastructure for diagnosis and treatment.

To tackle this problem, and to assist in the detection and diagnosis of COVID-19 in the underserved parts of the world, we have come up with an image classification model using deep learning which can detect COVID-19 from the Chest X-Ray scans with reasonably high accuracy (>95%). This model when deployed in areas where healthcare infrastructure is not up to the mark and testing is in limited numbers, can help to make X-Ray scans another alternative in the fight against COVID-19.
